Practices


Attending Practice

You can attend practice with any skill level and at any time throughout the year (see weekly practice schedule below). Most of our new members start with little to no archery experience, so don't be afraid to try it out! To attend club practices, you must do the following things:

  • Sign the waiver by becoming a member on SLI*
  • Pay membership dues* ($30/term, or to $70/year). You may attend your first two practices for free!
  • Sign up on the sign-up sheet and show up to practice!

*Note: becoming a member on SLI is seperate from paying membership dues. You must be a member (which includes signing the waiver) to come to practice, but you only need to pay dues after attending 2 practices.


Your first practice

  • As we practice outside, make sure to dress for the weather and wear closed-toe shoes
  • We provide all the necessary archery equipment! You are also welcome to use your own equipment, although please note that currently we cannot allow compound bows to shoot at the Equestrian Center due to safety concerns. We are working on resolving this, and compounds are welcome to shoot at EE Wilson!
  • At your first practice, make sure to check in with an officer so that they can perform safety training for you. This safety training ensures that members can practice archery safely and responsibly and follow range rules.

Fall 2025 Practice Schedule: (last updated 9/24/2025)

We practice on Sundays and on Tuesdays. Due to daylight savings time, we change our schedule a bit at the end of October.

October:

  • Sundays 11-1 and 1-3 in the horse arena at the Equestrian Center (attend whichever session you prefer)
  • Tuesdays 6-7:30 at EE Wilson
After October:
  • Sundays 11-1 in the horse arena at the Equestrian Center
  • Tuesdays 5:30-7:30 in the horse arena at the Equestrian Center

See calendar below!

For Horse Arena practices, we meet 15 minutes beforehand to carpool. For EE Wilson practices, we meet half an hour beforehand. Our first practice is Sunday, October 5.

Our on-campus carpool meeting spot is near Dryden Hall. If you are willing to help carpool please indicate how many seats you have available on the sign up sheet and bring your car to the meeting spot. You are also welcome to drive yourself or bike to the range.

Note: For the most specific and up-to-date times and meeting details, we recommend joining the Archery Club Discord or signing up for our mailing list by becoming a member.
